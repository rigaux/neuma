Users¶

The chapter describes the management or roles and access rights. Neuma recognizes four role: anonymous (not connected), visitor, editor and administrator.

Access rights operate at the corpus level. They can be split in view and edit. Editing means both creation, and modification (which includes the right to add opuses to a corpus), but not deletion.

Recall that a corpus has a visibility status which is either public or private. Access rights depend on both this status and on user’s role. The rules are essentially the following:

  • Anonymous users can see public corpuses, but cannot edit them ; they do not see private corpuses
